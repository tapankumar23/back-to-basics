How The System Knows A Shipment Is Inactive

Inference + Sensing
Core Principle

The control tower infers inactivity from missing expected events. If a shipment was last scanned at Hub A and no follow-up scan, sort, load, or transfer event arrives within the SLA window, a timer or stream rule marks the shipment as stale in the digital twin and emits an exception.

Rule example: if status = AT_HUB and now - last_scan_time > 12h and no downstream milestone exists, emit shipment.no_activity.detected.
State The Platform Tracks
  • last_scan_time and last_scan_location
  • current_status such as inbound, staged, or loaded
  • expected_next_event_by derived from hub SLA
  • last_physical_signal_time when sensors are available
How Physical Inactivity Is Verified
  • Mandatory operator scans at each hub handoff
  • RFID readers detecting zone entry and exit
  • BLE or UWB dwell-time tracking inside the hub
  • WMS bin, shelf, or staging-lane movement updates
Confidence Caveat
  • No new scan means no recorded activity, not guaranteed no movement
  • Sensor corroboration increases confidence that the shipment is physically lying in the hub
  • Unresolved stale cases should escalate to hub operations for manual validation

Low-Level Decision Sequence
1. Detect

Rule engine or stream processor checks `now - last_scan_time` against the allowed inactivity window.

2. Corroborate

Sensor and WMS signals confirm whether the shipment is still physically dwelling in Hub A or simply missing scans.

3. Diagnose

Agents evaluate congestion, handling backlog, route issues, carrier capacity, and customer impact.

4. Decide

Decision logic computes confidence and checks whether policy mandates manual approval.

5. Execute

Approved action bundles are dispatched and completion states feed back into metrics and the ledger.

Failure And Recovery Mechanics
  • Agent timeout triggers retry workflow and dead-letter retention.
  • Conflicting signals reduce confidence and force human review.
  • Execution adapters report success, rejection, or partial completion.
  • Every retry path appends new rationale to the audit record.
  • Replay is safe because event envelopes are durable and traceable.
Implementation Detail

Physical inactivity is an inference until corroborated by sensors, WMS movement logs, or manual validation.

Agent Responsibilities
  • Detection Agent: stale-scan and anomaly identification
  • Diagnosis Agent: root-cause hypotheses and supporting evidence
  • Decision Agent: action plan and confidence computation
  • Risk Agent: policy, compliance, and approval routing
  • Audit Agent: rationale and lineage persistence
Integration Interfaces
  • Inbound event APIs and stream consumers
  • Knowledge and policy retrieval endpoints
  • Enterprise action adapters for TMS and WMS
  • Notification services for customers and hub ops
  • Observability pipeline for KPIs and alerting
Non-Functional Concerns
  • Low-latency stream processing for exception detection
  • Traceability across every agent and action step
  • Replayability for audits and incident analysis
  • Graceful fallback when sensor data is incomplete
  • Human override for ambiguous or high-risk cases

Sequence Narrative
Stage 1

Source systems and physical sensors emit shipment handling, location, and dwell-time events.

Stage 2

The ingestion layer normalizes payloads and routes them onto the exception bus with correlation metadata.

Stage 3

The detector service updates the shipment digital twin and opens a case when the inactivity threshold is breached.

Stage 4

The agent mesh pulls operational context, validates policy, computes decision confidence, and records rationale.

Stage 5

The control tower routes the decision to auto-execution or human approval, then publishes outcomes into metrics and governance.

Decision Branches
  • High confidence plus policy-safe action goes directly to execution adapters.
  • Low confidence, conflicting signals, or risky customer impact forces human approval.
  • Execution failure triggers retries, dead-letter retention, and additional ledger entries.
  • Resolved outcomes close the feedback loop by updating telemetry, KPIs, and the shipment twin.

Shipment Twin

Stable identifier used to correlate OMS, WMS, TMS, sensor, and control tower records.

status / location

Latest business state and hub or transit position for the shipment.

last_scan_time / last_physical_signal_time

Used to distinguish missing digital events from verified physical inactivity.

expected_next_event_by

Time boundary derived from SLA, route milestone, and hub handling rules.

Exception Case
case_id / event_name

Unique exception instance and canonical event such as `shipment.no_activity.detected`.

reason_code / severity

Explains whether the case was caused by missing scans, hub dwell, sensor silence, or ambiguity.

evidence

Contains timestamps, hub context, sensor observations, and downstream milestone gaps.

confidence_score

Quantifies how safely the platform can decide or auto-execute.

Action Plan
recommended_actions

Structured list of reroute, escalate, notify, and manual-validation steps.

approval_required

Boolean gate determined by policy and confidence thresholds.

execution_status

Tracks queued, approved, dispatched, failed, and completed transitions.

Audit And Metrics

Stores decision lineage, contributing agents, policy checks, and action justification.

retry_count / dlq_status

Supports resilience reporting and replay-safe recovery.

latency / throughput / failure_rate

Operational KPIs used to monitor health of the control tower workflow.
